## Style Prompt

PaperSpine uses a precise AI research-product identity: a dark ink canvas, crisp document-grid structure, measured motion, and one warm amber accent that marks human choice and rationale. The video should feel like a transparent research workflow being opened up, not a generic tech promo.

## Colors

- Background: #101319, near-black ink for a focused technical canvas.
- Surface: #171d26, raised panels and matrix cells.
- Text: #f3efe7, warm off-white for readable Chinese typography.
- Muted text: #9aa6b2, secondary labels and process notes.
- Accent: #f6b73c, motivation, user choice, and key proof points.
- Support: #4fd1c5, adaptive routing and platform coverage.

## Typography

- Chinese and body text: Microsoft YaHei, Noto Sans SC, PingFang SC, system sans-serif.
- Latin labels and metric text: Inter, Segoe UI, Arial, sans-serif.
- Headlines use heavy weight and compact line height. Body stays regular, clean, and readable.

## Motion

- Grid-locked, deliberate, and explanatory.
- Entrances are short upward or lateral reveals with `power3.out`.
- Matrix rows and feature chips reveal in sequence so the viewer can read the workflow.
- Avoid chaotic particle effects, purple-blue gradients, and generic neon SaaS visuals.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not make it a celebratory vanity montage.
- Do not use a fixed-card grid that makes every feature look equal.
- Do not hide the teaching value behind decoration.
- Do not imply PaperSpine only polishes prose.
